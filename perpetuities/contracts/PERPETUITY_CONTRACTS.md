These contracts bind structure across time, not activity.

## Core Perpetuity Principles

1. **Reconstructable, not immortal**
2. **Readable before executable**
3. **Human authority persists beyond uptime**
4. **Structure outlives process**
5. **Failure defaults to silence, not action**

## Persistence Guarantees

The system guarantees:

- Canonical documents remain authoritative
- Structural intent is preserved verbatim
- No component gains authority through age
- No automation gains rights through longevity
- No agent self-elevates via continuity

---

## Drift Prohibition

The following are explicitly forbidden:

- Structural drift through automation
- Silent schema mutation
- Authority escalation over time
- Emergent behavior redefining scope
- Self-authorship of governance documents

Any detected drift triggers **fail-closed posture**.

---

## Time-Bound Authority

- No component gains authority by persistence
- Longevity does not imply trust
- Trust is explicitly granted, never accrued
- All authority traces back to human origin

---

## Dependency Lock

Perpetuity Contracts depend on:

- Continuity rules
- Ledger invariants
- Restore constraints
- Execution gates
- Human override priority

Violation of any dependency invalidates perpetuity claims.

---

## Terminal Clause

Perpetuity does **not** mean permanence.

It means:
- Surviving long enough to be understood
- Preserving structure until safely handed off
- Ending safely if reconstruction fails

Silence is preferred over corruption.
